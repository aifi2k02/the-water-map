About

Roughly a quarter of the world still lacks safely managed drinking water — water that is at home, available when needed, and free from contamination. The Water Map lets you explore where that gap is, country by country, and how it has shifted since the year 2000.

Defining water access

Safely managed

The highest standard: an improved water source on premises, available when needed, and free of contamination. This is the headline figure on the map.

At least basic

A lower bar: an improved source within a 30-minute round trip. Where a country has no “safely managed” figure, we show this instead.

How to read it

On the map, countries are shaded from pale (low access) to deep navy (high access); grey means no data. Click any country, or search by name, to open a panel with the headline figure, how many people that leaves without safe water, and a chart of the trend since 2000. You can overlay a second country to compare.

The “global average” and “without safe water” figures shown on the map are computed directly from this dataset (population-weighted), not pulled from a headline elsewhere.
